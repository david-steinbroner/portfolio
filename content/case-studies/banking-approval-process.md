Every feature that touched payments required approval from our banking partner. But the process was broken in ways that went beyond slow timelines.

---

## The Problem

What was actually happening: our compliance team would submit materials, but they didn't fully understand Bitcoin or our product. Banking partners would see something in the live app that didn't match what they'd approved. We'd get a call. Everything would stop. Engineers would scramble that day to change things. Roadmap items sat in limbo for 4+ weeks while async back-and-forth dragged on.

The real issue wasn't just speed - it was that nobody owned the full picture. There was no single source of truth for what the app actually did, no process for surfacing changes before they shipped, and no relationship where partners felt informed rather than surprised.

---

## What I Built

I took ownership of a problem that wasn't technically mine to fix.

First, I mapped the entire app - every feature, every flow, every compliance-relevant touchpoint. This became the single source of truth that hadn't existed before.

Then I rebuilt the workflow:

- Established direct relationships with banking partner contacts, bypassing the account management layers that were slowing communication
- Became the storyteller for our product to partners. Our compliance person didn't understand Bitcoin well enough to explain why something was safe. I did. So I took over that translation.
- Created a forward-looking process where partners always knew what was coming before it shipped. Any updates to the app were marked, submitted, and explained in advance.
- Built feedback loops into the timeline so partners could flag concerns while we were still in design - not after launch
- Set up regular check-ins instead of async back-and-forth, which let me address questions in real-time and adjust specs on the spot

Finally, I took what I learned from partners and built an internal compliance guide for our teams. Designers and engineers now knew what would and wouldn't pass approval before they built it. We stopped designing things that were out of compliance.

---

## The Impact

- Reduced approval timeline by 75% - from 4+ weeks to days
- Eliminated surprise scrambles - partners never saw something in the app they hadn't already approved
- Made compliance a non-blocker - approval became a checkpoint, not a bottleneck
- Unblocked multiple roadmap items that had been stuck in approval limbo
- Created institutional knowledge - the compliance guide and templates meant this process would survive without me

---

## What I Learned

Own the translation layer. In regulated industries, the person who can tell the product story to compliance partners in their language is the person who unblocks the roadmap. I spent as much time selling the product story to banking partners as I did to users - but I told it completely differently.

Process problems are relationship problems. The 75% reduction wasn't about better templates. It was about partners trusting that I'd keep them informed, so they could move fast when I needed them to.

Fix the system, not the symptom. The obvious fix was "make approvals faster." The real fix was making sure we never designed things that would fail approval in the first place.
