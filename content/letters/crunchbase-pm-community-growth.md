My four years at Fold came in two halves: I spent the first two building the community, then the next two as a product manager turning it into a growth and roadmap engine. Community manager to PM, same instinct the whole way - listen, then build. That's the loop Crunchbase is hiring for across 80 million users, and I've run it end to end.

I was employee #11 there, and helped scale Fold - the bitcoin rewards app that grew into a full financial ecosystem - to a Nasdaq listing. I inherited a dormant community and grew it to ~45,000 members on under $5K a month, no paid acquisition. I built Friends of Fold, a 40-plus power-user group that became the advisory council, beta cohort, and early-access list this role describes, then promoted trusted members to help run it so it carried itself. When I put the Discord link in-app next to "submit a ticket," about 25% of taps went to the community instead of support.

Running it taught me feedback doesn't all weigh the same - a Twitter poll is a gut reaction, an email poll is a sharper signal from fewer people, a Discord thread tells you the why. I ran all those channels myself and wired them - plus support and the app store - into Coda (our doc hub) through Zapier, then used AI to cluster by volume and sentiment and turn it into specs the team could act on. Crunchbase's channels will weigh differently, and learning them is the first job.

Our data said premium members were getting some of the best rewards in the market, but talking to them, it didn't feel that way. So we worked with product design to surface the value - showing how much their bitcoin rewards had grown, adding a crossed-out fee line on every transaction so they saw the fees they weren't paying. Sentiment jumped, and so did transaction volume. The same listening pushed me to rebuild our rewards wheel so free users could see the prizes they were locked out of - a new upgrade entry point that lifted conversions 8% in its first month.

The same signal-reading is how I ended up owning Fold's positioning. I took over marketing as we scaled and wrote the tagline it still uses - "personal finance powered by bitcoin" - the angle that moved us from growing users to getting paid by them.

I built the paid side too: an affiliate program where partners earned only when a referral actually bought or spent, never on the signup, so it rewarded real usage over vanity numbers. Some months it drove 20% of our new growth.

These days I prototype live, and AI makes it fast - I'll build a change in Claude Code or mock a flow in Figma and adjust it on the fly. At Fold I did that during community sessions, not in a follow-up, because I knew the product cold and was easy in the room. So when someone surfaces a need in an AMA, I can build the real thing in front of them and ship it - a few of those builds are at davidsteinbroner.com.

You're building a feedback engine to power the future of private-market intelligence. I've built the smaller version of that, end to end. I'd like to build yours.
